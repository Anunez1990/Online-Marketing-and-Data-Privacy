Edward Snowden

November 20th, 2020 by Aayush Kumar

  • Introduction

    Edward Joseph Snowden who was a former National Security Agency (N.S.A) subcontractor turned into a whistle-blower(someone who exposes information or activity within a private, public, or government organization that is deemed illegal, or fraud).

    In his case, it was releasing information detailing the highly illegal ‘data mining’ practices of the N.S.A have caused shockwaves throughout the world and have raised important questions concerning how much the government actually monitors its people without their consent or knowledge.


    Exposing the government

    Edward Snowden was a computer expert and after leaving the N.S.A was a Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) employee and subcontractor In 2013, he released confidential government documents to the press about the existence of government surveillance programs.

    Edward Snowden did not release sensitive data onto the Internet for everyone to see. He carefully examined the the documents and data he collected to make sure that the leaks released contained only that which pertained to the N.S.A’s illegal collecting of phone logs, user data, and other private information.


    Hero or Traitor

    The release of classified government documents did spark a controversial debates. The documents reveal N.S.A.’s secret surveillance program which is allowing them to spy on the things we browse on the internet and the people we call and text . As a result, millions of U.S. citizens are angry of the actions taking place without their consent. Edward Snowden is a hero to the Americans as he is fighting for their 4th Amendment right.

    But at the same time, his actions has jeopardized America’s safety putting them in a vulnerable position.
