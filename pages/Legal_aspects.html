Legal Aspects of Online Privacy

November 19th, 2020 by Aayush Kumar

  • Introduction

    Hackers/ scammers try to take advantage of stealing information on the internet, and misusing it for wrong purposes. There are many federal and state acts that are put into place to ensure safety on the online world. Precautions must be also taken by us to ensure safety.


    Internet privacy laws

    The potential for breaches of online privacy has grown significantly over the years. There is no single law regulating online privacy, but there are federal and state laws that are applied Some key federal laws affecting online privacy are:

    Federal Trade Commission Act:This regulates unfair or deceptive commercial practices. This deals with companies that fail to comply with posted privacy policies.
    Electronic Communications Privacy:protects certain wire, oral, and electronic communications from unauthorized interception, access, use, and disclosure
    Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act:requires certain website and online service providers to obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting, using, or disclosing personal information from minors under the age of 13.
    Fair and Accurate Credit Transactions Act:requires financial institutions and creditors to maintain written identity theft prevention programs.


    Your threat and what to do

    Client, customer, and employees can be subject to a data breach for access to their personal information in many ways. Scammers and Hacker try to gain information from you such as e-mail addresses, banking, passwords, physical addresses, phone numbers and more.
