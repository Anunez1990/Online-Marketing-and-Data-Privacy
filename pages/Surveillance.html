Surveillance capitalism

November 21th, 2020 by Aayush Kumar

  • Introduction

    Surveillance capitalism was invented by Google, with the development of online advertising in the early 2000s. This is a type of system through which data of customers/people are collected and sold to buyers who would use it to gain profit. Surveillance capitalists know everything about us, whereas their use of our data are designed to be unknown to us.

    We allow ourselves to give information about us to these surveillance capitalists by unknown actions like playing games, or posting our pictures on social media. We will see different ways in which Surveillance capitalism is carried out in this article.


    Pokemon-Go

    Pokemon Go is a reality mobile game that requires the users to go searching around in real world to find pokemon. But, this is not just an ordinary game, and there is a lot more to it. This game gave its creators the chance to run a global, digital experiment on how to influence real-world behaviour for their own gain

    The mobile phone game places digital creatures(pokemon) in real-world locations and players have to try to find and catch them using GPS and augmented reality software. In choosing where to place the Pokemons in the real world, the company was able to "herd game players to specific places: establishments, bars, restaurants, pizza joints, service establishments, places where they might fix your car, or retail shops,"

    So essentially, the developers of the game are able to "sell the herd" to a buyer. This is surveillance capitalism where user data is being stole and sold to buyers without the player's knowledge.


    Facebook, Google and others

    Our lives and habits are being tracked. Every time we tap in a Google search, every time we click “Like” on Facebook and every time we make an Amazon purchase, we are giving away information about ourselves: our thoughts, our preferences, our behaviors.

    We are providing data that can be stored and sold to companies, which then use that data to try to modify our behavior and steer us toward buying their products and services. At this stage, we are basically a marketable product.


    Conclusion

    The surveillance capitalists are trying to do is sell predictions. It’s not only to know our behavior, but it’s to predict our behavior. That’s how they make their money. The worst part about this is that we don't know who these surveillance capitalists are selling the information to, and could result in a bad output if not monitored closely.
