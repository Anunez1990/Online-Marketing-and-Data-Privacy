Online Tracking

November 20th, 2020 by Aayush Kumar

  • Introduction

    Whenever we are doing anything online, we are being tracked. Our information is collected, and sold to buyers who use it for marketing purposes.


    How is User Data Collected?

    The most common technique to collect information about us are through "cookies". Cookies are tiny files placed in the computer by your browser when websites are visited. There are 2 basic types of cookies:

    Session Cookies:
    This type of cookies are good. All they do is enable us to easily move from one part of the website to another. These types of cookies are are only stored as long as the current session is active, and automatically deletes.

    Persistent Cookies:
    This type of cookies have a place in the computer and stay there. They are mostly used by marketing firms to track browsing history, and provide ads based off of what you search, and the pages you visit.

    This can be good in a sense that the relevancy of the ads may be welcome, but can also be intrusive. Another mean by which user data is collected is called "browser FingerPrints" which uses Javascript to collect our information. More info about browser fingerprints can be found here


    What do companies know about us?

    The basic information that are collected about us include: Gender, age, personal statutes, address/ current location. Other than this,they know what type of music we like, favorite TV shows/ movies, where we buy online and what. Using all this information, individual profiles are created about us, which provides precise conclusions about us.

    These profiles on us are worth a lot of money and are being sold. The types of organizations that are interested in our data are banks, landlords, and insurers. This information helps them assess how reliable a customer might pay, and the marketing industry uses this data to generate personalized ads.


    How to Prevent Tracking

    Completely avoiding tracking is nearly impossible, but reducing the amount of information that's being given away can be done. More information about maximizing online security can be found here
