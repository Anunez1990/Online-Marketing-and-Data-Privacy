Maximize security online

November 20th, 2020 by Aayush Kumar

  • Introduction

    Every time you browse the Internet, your privacy is under constant threat from cybercriminals, governments, and corporations who want to get their hands on your personal information. At a basic level your privacy is constantly being invaded by the sites you visit, your ISP(Internet service provider), the government, and even services used like ‘Google’ or ‘Facebook’. The main purpose of stealing personal data, is for selling the data to third party marketers for their own purpose.

    Doxing and other types of leaks of personal info are also a possibility.It becomes our responsibility as well to protect our privacy and personal space on the Internet by the use of various methods discussed below.


    Basic Privacy

    Passwords/ 2 Factor Authentication:

    Having a strong password is very important and a password manager is highly recommended. Never answer security questions online truthfully, so that its harder for anyone else to guess, and access your info. Having a two-factor-authentication with either a phone number or google authenticator, which is a better alternative. Also avoid using default passwords like the ones provided on routers/internet modems.

    Web Browser/extensions:

    There is no issue with using different known web-browsers, however it is important to use trusted add-ons/ extension and nothing sketchy. Never store cookies, history, cash and if done so, clear them frequently.
    Having a VPN is essential to being secure, it hides the user's identity from websites visited, ISP, and any other person trying to track the user. Avoid free VPN and proxies.

    Social Media:

    Try not to use real information like names, addresses, phone numbers etc.
    Don't talk to strangers which includes not posting in public comments.
    Disable unnecessary permissions such as locations, sms text messages,and other permissions, that certain apps might not require.

    Smartphone/ Computer:

    Basic security for smartphones and computers are to have an active VPN at all times, disable permissions/ location services and bluetooth as frequently as possible, and dont have unnecessary apps.


    Complex Privacy

    Communication

    Texting/SMS are very insecure, the best is to use "I-message" which only works on apple devices or use "Signal", which is one of the most secure messengers as it is open-sourced and fully encrypted. "Signal" can be set up as a default SMS app on android. Apple doesn't offer this feature, but it can still be run as a separate app. "Signal" also protects phone call privacy.

    Email

    Most email providers completely invade all emails sent back and forward. "ProtonMail" is a secure email service, and offers security the same way as "Signal". It is open-sourced and fully encrypted, which prevents any third party from viewing emails sent among two people.

    Storage

    The hardware's content needs to be periodically cleaned up using a tool called "CCleaner", which permanently deletes unwanted files. Clearing data that is not needed. This is a good practice to prevent possibility of people who do manage to get hands on your software.

    Web Browsers

    Avoid using chrome or any chrome-based browsers since google is known for collecting data. Instead use Firefox or any other privacy based browser. For extensions use "uBlock Origin" which is a privacy oriented ad blocker; "Privacy Badger" a tracker blocker and "HTTPS Everywhere" to keep using https instead of http.


    Extreme Privacy

    Extreme privacy is not required, but these are basics of how to be extremely secure. Linux is more secure than windows, this is why the safest Operating system is a fully installed Linux distribution or a Linux host OS.
